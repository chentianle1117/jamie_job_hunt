**Thesis:** How to overcome "Design Fixation" when using generative AI in early-stage industrial design.

**Core Argument:** AI tools force designers into a "shopping" posture (browsing high-fidelity outputs) rather than a "designing" posture (exploring conceptual intent). Current latent space interfaces (Form Forge, BLS, DeepRise, Runway) provide mathematical control but not semantic control.

**Proposed Solution:**
1. **Semantic Canvas** — spatial layout for lineage tracking, avoiding fixation by showing explored/unexplored regions
2. **Brief-Aware Agent** — clarifies evolving design intent, steers exploration, names emerging directions

**Testbed:** Footwear design at HILOS startup. Shoes have highly coupled product architectures (vamp must mate with midsole), making them the ultimate stress-test for generative models.

**Methodology:** Research through Design (RtD) — David is embedded at HILOS, building tools and negotiating design semantics with real designers.
