**Strategy:** "Gradual move-over" — Jamie leads, David follows 1-2 years later.

**Why:** Jamie has been in a toxic, non-sponsoring job for ~2-3 years. Mental health is a priority over staying in the US. David has an ideal job at HILOS but can't drag Jamie through more of the same.

**Visa Relay Plan:**
1. **Jamie's Orientation Year (Zoekjaar):** She is within 3 years of her master's graduation — applies now. If approved, she goes to Netherlands to explore and job-hunt while David provides economic stability from Portland.
2. **David's Orientation Year:** He graduates CMU May 2026 → has until ~2029 to apply his own Orientation Year. If needed, David can be primary applicant and Jamie re-joins as spouse — extending their window by another year.
3. **HSM conversion:** Once either of them lands a Dutch employer as Recognised Sponsor, they convert to Highly Skilled Migrant. 5 years → permanent residency (no lottery, unlike US green card).

**David's role during transition:** Stay at HILOS in Portland, do excellent work, build AI pipeline experience and irreplaceability. Explore remote/contractor arrangement with HILOS when ready to move. Realistic: HILOS is an in-person West Coast startup with 9-hour time diff to NL — contractor is possible but not ideal long-term. David will job-search in Netherlands ConTech/Design Engineering market independently ~1-2 years after Jamie lands.

**Jamie's exit conditions from current job:** FMLA leave runs through ~June 2026. Decision point: go to Netherlands vs. return to toxic job. Current lean = Netherlands.

**Exit cost acknowledged:** Leaving the US is essentially irreversible given Trump-era visa climate (10K tariff on foreign hires), David's Chinese citizenship (no lottery path to green card), and no desire to return to mainland China. The "exit cost is high" framing is real — but staying costs Jamie's wellbeing.

**Why:** Preserving Jamie's mental health and marriage stability outweighs David's career momentum in the US. Netherlands offers lower career ceiling but much higher floor — no healthcare bankruptcy risk, mandatory 25+ days leave, strong育儿 (childcare/parenting) infrastructure, realistic PR path.
