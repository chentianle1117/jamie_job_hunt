## Header Identity Line

Jamie consistently brands herself with a two-line tagline beneath her name. The tagline follows a fixed formula:

**Line 1 (always identical):**
> "A Solution-focused, Data-driven, and People-oriented Professional"

**Line 2 (varies by role focus):**
- L&D / talent development roles: "Dedicated to Driving Individual and Organizational Development"
- Employee experience / engagement roles: "Dedicated to Improving Employee Experience" or "Dedicated to Improving People Experience"

This tagline is a stable identity anchor — she does NOT change it opportunistically for every company.

## Opening Hook Patterns

Jamie opens every cover letter with a **reframe sentence** that challenges the obvious interpretation of the job function, then pivots to her deeper philosophy.

**Formula:**
> "[Role domain] isn't just about [surface-level expectation]—from my experience, it's about [deeper/systemic reality]."

**Verbatim examples (all 5 letters):**

1. (Jamf / PCC — L&D focus):
> "Employee development isn't just about delivering training—from my experience, it's about deeply understanding employee needs through ground-up feedback, and continuously refining strategies based on measurable impact."

2. (Instacart / Flatiron / Roblox — EX focus):
> "Employee experience isn't just about engagement activities—from my experience, it's about creating a continuous cycle of collecting insights, analyzing data, and refining solutions that drive meaningful improvement."

**Pattern notes:**
- Always starts with the function noun, not "I"
- The em dash is load-bearing — it's how she creates tension and then resolution in one sentence
- "from my experience" is a signature hedge that grounds the philosophical claim in lived practice
- Opening never name-drops the company in sentence 1 — company appears at end of paragraph 1

---

## Opening Paragraph Structure

Para 1 always has three beats:
1. Reframe hook (above)
2. Background credential statement: "With a background in [X, Y, and Z], I have helped organizations [specific outcome]."
3. Forward-looking bridge: "I look forward to bringing [strength cluster] to [Company] as a [exact title]."

**Background credential always cites 3 domains.** Common triples:
- HR, Organizational Development, Partnerships Management (L&D-focused)
- HR, Organizational Development, Program Management (EX-focused)
