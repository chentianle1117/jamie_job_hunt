## ✅ Part 1: Review each APPLICATION (the most important part)

For **each of the 5 submitted applications**, click to expand and check these 5 things:

### 1. Resume — is anything wrong or made up?
- [ ] **Open the embedded resume PDF.** Read every bullet.
- [ ] **Are all the numbers real?** (e.g., "75% ramp-up reduction", "600+ participants", "20+ programs", "3 global locations"). If any number is inflated or invented, flag it — this is the #1 thing we must never get wrong.
- [ ] **Is the job title framing right?** Each resume is tailored — does the summary line match what you'd actually call yourself?
- [ ] **Does it stay on one page?** (It should — verify visually.)

> **How to give feedback:** "The Built In resume says X but it should say Y" or "Never use the phrase '___' — sounds fake" or "The 78% number is wrong, it was enrollment not adoption."

### 2. Cover letter — does it sound like you?
- [ ] **Open the embedded cover letter PDF.** Read it out loud in your head.
- [ ] **Does it sound like AI wrote it?** If yes — that's a red flag, tell us to simplify.
- [ ] **Is the opening hook good?** (Each starts with "X isn't just about Y—from my experience, it's about Z." Do you like that pattern, or is it overused now?)
- [ ] **Any cliches that make you cringe?** ("drove strategic transformation", "passionate about", etc.)

> **How to give feedback:** "Cover letters are too formal" / "too eager" / "the hook is getting repetitive, vary it" / "I like the Roivant one best, do more like that."

### 3. Form fields — did we fill the application correctly?
- [ ] Click **"Exact Form Fields Submitted."**
- [ ] **Email = jamiecheng0103@gmail.com?** ✓
- [ ] **Phone = +1-213-700-3831?** ✓
- [ ] **Sponsorship = Yes?** (We always answer truthfully — you need H1B. Confirm this is right.)
- [ ] **Location / relocation answers** — are you actually open to NYC, Chicago, Mountain View? If NOT, tell us which cities to stop applying to.
- [ ] **Demographics** — we just switched to Woman + Asian (was "I don't wish to answer"). Are you OK with this? (See Part 4.)

> **How to give feedback:** "Stop saying I'll relocate to NYC, I won't" or "the YOE answer should be 2-3 not 3" or "demographics: keep declining actually."

### 4. Screenshot — proof it submitted
- [ ] Click **"Submission Screenshot"** — you'll see the confirmation page or final state. This is proof the application actually went through.

### 5. Was this role even worth applying to?
- [ ] Look at the role + location + salary in the card header.
- [ ] **Would you actually take this job?** If a role is a bad fit (wrong location, too junior, wrong function), tell us so we stop surfacing similar ones.

> **How to give feedback:** "Aurora HR Generalist is too admin-y, I want more strategic roles" or "Mountain View is too far, Portland/Seattle/remote only" or "these are all good, keep going."

## 📧 Part 2: Review each OUTREACH draft

For **each of the 5 outreach emails**, click to expand and check:

- [ ] **Read the full email body** (shown inline in the dashboard).
- [ ] **Is the tone right?** We deliberately varied them — formal for the SVP (Kelly), casual for the alum (Kaitlyn), curious/low-pressure for the cold contact (Valerie). Tell us which tones land and which feel off.
- [ ] **Is the recipient right?** Each has a LinkedIn link — click to verify they're a real, relevant person. (We already caught that Jessica Redeman left Roivant and replaced her with Emily Formea.)
- [ ] **Is the email address right?** Most are educated guesses (e.g., `kelly.graff@roivant.com`). Verify before sending.
- [ ] **Should we send these at all?** You may prefer LinkedIn messages over cold email, or prefer not to do outreach for certain companies.

> **How to give feedback:** "The Kelly Graff one is too formal, warm it up" / "Don't cold-email SVPs, LinkedIn only" / "I love the Amber one, that's my voice" / "Don't reach out to Roivant people, I'd rather not."

**To actually send one:** Open Gmail → search `[OUTREACH]` → the draft is there with the recipient filled in → review → click Send. (We never auto-send.)

## ⚙️ Part 3: The mechanism — how aggressive should we be?

The pipeline can now auto-submit to: **Greenhouse, PinpointHQ, and LinkedIn Easy Apply.** It cannot do Workday, iCIMS, or PeopleAdmin (bot-blocked).

Questions for you:
- [ ] **Volume vs. selectivity?** Do you want more applications (cast wide) or fewer, higher-quality ones? Tonight the market was thin — only 5 truly good-fit roles existed. We can lower the bar for volume, or keep it strict.
- [ ] **Re-applying to the same company?** We applied to Aurora twice (People PM + HR Generalist). Is that OK, or one-app-per-company?
- [ ] **Portland-local emphasis?** Most roles tonight were out-of-state (NYC, Chicago, SF). Want us to weight Portland/Seattle harder, even if it means fewer total applications?
- [ ] **Auto-submit vs. review-first?** Right now we auto-submit when confidence is high. Do you want to review every package BEFORE it submits, or trust the pipeline and review after?

> **How to give feedback:** "Review everything before submitting" or "Auto-submit is fine, I trust it" or "Portland + remote only from now on" or "Quality over quantity, max 3 per night."

## 📝 How to actually deliver your feedback

Easiest: **just talk to David** (or type to the assistant) in plain language. You don't need to be structured. Examples of perfect feedback:

> "Cover letters are a bit too long, tighten them. The Roivant one is the right length. Stop applying to Mountain View — too far. Demographics, keep Woman + Asian, that's fine. Don't send the Kelly Graff email, too formal for me. Send the Amber and Valerie ones. For Anthropic, do the warm intro. And going forward, review packages with me before submitting — I want to see them first for the next few."

That paragraph alone would calibrate the entire pipeline. The more specific the better, but even "these all look good, keep going but Portland-first" is useful.
