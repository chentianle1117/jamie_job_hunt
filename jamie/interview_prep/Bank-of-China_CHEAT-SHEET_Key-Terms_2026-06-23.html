PART 1 — US EMPLOYMENT LAW (the JD says "Knowledge of Employment Law/EEOC required")


You won't be quizzed like a lawyer. They want: do you know these exist, roughly what they protect, and do you think in terms of compliance/fairness/documentation. Recognize → one sentence each.

TermWhat it is (say it like this)
EEOCEqual Employment Opportunity Commission — the federal agency that enforces anti-discrimination laws in employment. The umbrella behind most of the below.
Title VII (Civil Rights Act 1964)Bans employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin. The foundational one. Covers hiring, firing, pay, promotion.
ADAAmericans with Disabilities Act — bans discrimination against people with disabilities; requires reasonable accommodation.
ADEAAge Discrimination in Employment Act — protects workers 40+ from age discrimination. (Bonus — good to know it exists.)
FLSAFair Labor Standards Act — governs minimum wage, overtime, exempt vs. non-exempt classification, child labor. The "wage & hour" law.
FMLAFamily and Medical Leave Act — up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave for medical/family reasons (50+ employee firms).
At-will employmentDefault US rule: either party can end employment anytime, for any legal reason (not an illegal one like discrimination/retaliation).
I-9 / E-VerifyI-9 = the form verifying an employee's identity + legal authorization to work in the US (everyone fills one). E-Verify = the electronic system that checks I-9 data against govt records. Big deal for record retention + audits.
EEO-1 reportAnnual demographic workforce report some employers must file with the EEOC. (Recognize it.)
Record retentionLegal requirement to keep certain HR/employment records for set periods (e.g., I-9s, payroll, hiring records) and dispose of them properly when the period ends. ← This is literally in your JD.
NY-specific (nice to drop)New York has its own strong layer: NY State Human Rights Law, NYC Human Rights Law, pay-transparency law (salary ranges in postings — note BOC posted one!), sexual-harassment-prevention training mandate.

If asked "what employment laws are you familiar with?": "Through my HR program and my HRBP work — Title VII and the EEOC anti-discrimination framework, ADA accommodation, FLSA wage-and-hour and exempt/non-exempt, FMLA, I-9/E-Verify for work authorization, and at-will employment. In New York specifically there's the added State and City Human Rights Law layer and pay-transparency rules. My formal depth is still growing, but the framework is familiar and I'm studying it right now."



PART 2 — BANKING RISK & COMPLIANCE (why this role exists)


TermWhat it is (say it like this)
Three Lines of DefenseThe risk-management model banks use. 1st line = the front-line business unit that owns and runs its own controls (← HR is a 1st line for its own processes; this QC unit is how HR proves its controls work). 2nd line = independent Risk & Compliance that sets standards and monitors. 3rd line = Internal Audit, fully independent assurance. Memorize this — it's your #1 credibility line.
OCCOffice of the Comptroller of the Currency — the federal regulator that charters and examines national banks and federal branches of foreign banks. BOC's NY branch is OCC-supervised.
Federal Reserve (Fed)Central bank + supervises bank holding companies and foreign banking organizations operating in the US.
NYDFSNew York Dept. of Financial Services — NY's state financial regulator; aggressive on AML/sanctions for banks operating in NY.
AMLAnti-Money Laundering — programs/controls to detect and prevent laundering illicit money through the bank.
BSABank Secrecy Act — the core US AML law; requires banks to keep records + file reports that help detect money laundering. "BSA/AML" travel together.
OFACOffice of Foreign Assets Control — enforces economic sanctions; banks must screen against sanctioned-party lists.
SARSuspicious Activity Report — the filing a bank submits to FinCEN when it spots potentially suspicious transactions. (BOC's 2018 penalty was partly for late SARs.)
KYCKnow Your Customer — verifying customer identity/risk. (Bonus.)
KRIKey Risk Indicator — a metric tracked to give early warning that risk is rising (e.g., # of overdue policy reviews, # of incomplete I-9s, training-completion rates). ← You'd monitor HR's KRIs. Different from a KPI (performance); a KRI signals risk.
ABACAnti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption — controls preventing bribery/corruption (gifts, conflicts of interest, improper payments). The JD says you'd coordinate ABAC controls for HR.
ControlA process/rule put in place to prevent or detect a risk (e.g., "two-person review before records are disposed").
Control testingChecking whether a control is actually working as designed — sample the records/steps, compare what people actually do vs. what the policy requires, document gaps, recommend corrective action, then re-test. ← This is the core of your job. Your Vestas/InGenius audit work IS this, just not in banking terms.
Risk assessmentSystematically identifying what could go wrong in a process, how likely, how bad, and what controls mitigate it.
Corrective actionThe fix applied when a control gap/failure is found.
Continuous monitoringOngoing checking (vs. one-time) that controls keep working and vendors/processes stay compliant.
Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM)The full vendor lifecycle as a risk discipline: planning → due diligence → contract → ongoing/continuous monitoring → termination. ← Your 10+ vendor relationships map directly.
Record retention scheduleThe documented policy of how long each record type is kept and when it's disposed. You'd maintain HR's.
Nepotism controlsRules preventing conflicts of interest from hiring/managing relatives — the JD names "Head Office nepotism-related requirements."
Audit / regulatory examWhen Internal Audit (3rd line) or a regulator (OCC/Fed/NYDFS) reviews the bank; HR has to produce clean deliverables and responses. You'd support these.
Recovery & Resolution Plan ("living will")Big-bank plan for how it'd wind down safely in a crisis; the JD mentions HR attends related meetings. (Just recognize it.)
Data Governance CommitteeCross-bank body overseeing data quality/handling; HR participates (record retention ties in).
Front Line Unit (FLU) Risk ManagerThe person in a 1st-line unit who owns its risk tasks; you'd assist HR's.
