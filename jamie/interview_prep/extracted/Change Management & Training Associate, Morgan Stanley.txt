Model Cheat Sheet
 
ADKAR (individual-level change — Prosci)
·       A — Awareness of the need for change
·       D — Desire to support and participate
·       K — Knowledge of how to change
·       A — Ability to implement new skills and behaviors
·       R — Reinforcement to sustain the change 
Kübler-Ross Change Curve (emotional response to change) 
9.     Shock/Denial — "This doesn't apply to me" / "It won't actually happen"
10.  Anger/Frustration — "Why are they doing this to us?"
11.  Bargaining — "Maybe I can keep doing things the old way for now"
12.  Depression — "This is overwhelming, I can't keep up"
13.  Acceptance — "Okay, I need to figure this out"
14.  (Extended) Experimentation — trying new behaviors
15.  (Extended) Integration — the new way is now normal

GenAI Risks — Practitioner POV
 
Your honest authenticity hook:

The 5 main risks to know:
29.  Hallucinations — AI confidently generates false info. In finance: incorrect regulatory refs, fabricated data, made-up product specs.
30.  Bias — training data biases in outputs. Stereotyped language in client comms; uneven recommendations.
31.  Data privacy — users pasting confidential client data into external tools. Why MS builds internal tools, but users still need training.
32.  Over-reliance — skipping critical thinking because "AI said so." Especially dangerous in regulated industries where advisors are legally accountable.
33.  Regulatory exposure — incorrect AI outputs could create SEC/FINRA violations.
 
How I'd Approach GenAI Risk Training
 
34.  Preface every training with context — before teaching how to use a tool, teach what it can and CAN'T do
35.  Use real examples of failures — actual hallucination samples, bias examples, privacy near-misses (memorable > abstract)
36.  Build guardrails into the content itself — "always verify X," "never paste Y," "flag Z"
37.  Teach escalation — when to go to compliance, when to stop using the tool
38.  Partner with legal/compliance SMEs — they own the risk content, I own the pedagogy
