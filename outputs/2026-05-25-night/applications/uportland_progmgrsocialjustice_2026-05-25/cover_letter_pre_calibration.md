My work has always been about creating conditions where people can develop — and at its best, that development happens in the context of community, shared values, and an honest reckoning with the systems that shape opportunity. The Program Manager, Social Justice Education role at the University of Portland sits in that territory, and it's a space I want to work in.

In my experience, the operational and the relational are inseparable in community program work. At InGenius Prep, I managed 20+ programs while holding the listening dimension: facilitating stakeholder sessions, conducting needs assessments, and building programming around what communities actually asked for rather than what we assumed they needed. The process of running those programs — communications strategy, vendor coordination, documentation that made the work repeatable — was infrastructure in service of connection.

At Vestas Wind Systems, I initiated and piloted an Inclusive Leadership workshop with senior leaders that was eventually expanded to 23 global locations. What made it land was the relational groundwork: understanding what those leaders were grappling with, building trust, and designing an experience that created real dialogue. That same orientation — showing up for the hard conversations, not just facilitating them — is what I'd bring to social justice programming.

I want to be honest: my primary background is in organizational development and HR, not academic social justice facilitation. I see this as a genuine learning opportunity, not just a skills transfer. University of Portland's mission and the Catholic Social Teaching foundation of this work are draws precisely because they ask for that kind of whole-person investment.

Thank you for your consideration.
