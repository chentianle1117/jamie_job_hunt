Dear EY People Consulting Team,

When I read that this role puts Humans@Center of the transformation agenda—designing organizations at the operating-model level so structure actually serves strategy—I felt genuinely drawn in. My graduate training in Applied Organizational Psychology taught me to treat an organization as a system to be studied: diagnose before designing, ask questions before assuming, and build in feedback loops so course correction stays cheap. That is the lens I would bring to EY's organization design work.

The work closest to this role is what I do through Organization Development Network (ODN) Oregon, where I'm part of a team of consultants supporting clients on real organizational challenges. For an NGO leadership team, we ran focus groups and stakeholder interviews to diagnose where decision-making and accountability were breaking down, then challenged the leaders to redesign those structures. For an HR client, we facilitated CEO and HR discovery sessions to diagnose the drivers behind absenteeism, analyzed the leave data to quantify $362K in costs, and turned it into executive-ready insights that led to policy redesigns. This is assessment-through-recommendation work—the same arc your team uses to guide clients from diagnosis to implementation.

That diagnostic instinct runs through the rest of my background. In a manager-effectiveness initiative at NextGen Healthcare, I analyzed 2,000+ employee responses, identified the driver that mattered most, and turned it into a report that reached the C-suite and shaped their strategy—leadership kept referencing those findings even after I left. At Vestas Wind Systems, I helped facilitate organizational change by coordinating across cross-functional stakeholders on a Mergers & Acquisitions project and by redesigning onboarding across three global locations, and I piloted an Inclusive Leadership program that scaled from 12 leaders to 23 sites by building awareness, finding internal champions, and reinforcing the change over time.

I'll be candid: this role asks for more years and more large-scale org-design project experience than I currently have, and I have not yet done this inside a global consultancy. What I can offer is genuine depth in organizational diagnosis, stakeholder facilitation, and data-driven recommendation, grounded in an MS in Applied Organizational Psychology, plus a track record of walking into ambiguity and leaving with frameworks. I don't just execute—I ask the right questions, find the root causes, and design solutions that stick. I'm energized by EY's commercial, sector-driven approach to people problems, and I would grow quickly in an environment that treats organizational structure as something to be engineered, not merely managed.

Thank you for reading. I would welcome the chance to show how a systems-minded, data-driven approach to organization design could contribute to EY's People Consulting practice.

Sincerely,
