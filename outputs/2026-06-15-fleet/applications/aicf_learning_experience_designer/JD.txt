Learning Experience Designer
American Indian College Fund
Location: Denver, CO (Hybrid)
Reports to: People Operations Director
Travel: No more than 10%
Compensation: $64,500 - $79,600 annually

ORGANIZATION OVERVIEW
The American Indian College Fund is the largest and highest-rated American Indian nonprofit organization, dedicated to increasing higher education access for Native students. Since its founding, the College Fund has distributed over $237.1 million in scholarships and program support and serves more than 4,000 Native students annually, helping Native scholars and Tribal colleges and universities access the education they need to flourish.

POSITION SUMMARY
The Learning Experience Designer designs, develops, and delivers engaging, learner-centered learning experiences that advance the College Fund's mission and measurable business outcomes. The role conducts needs assessments and gap analyses, partners with stakeholders to establish measurable learning objectives tied to business outcomes, and consults on instructional design principles and methodology. The designer integrates multiple learning channels (instructor-led, experiential, and self-paced) and designs innovative curricula that apply adult learning theory and emerging technologies, including generative AI tools.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
- Conduct needs assessments and gap analyses to identify learning needs and performance gaps.
- Collaborate with subject-matter experts and stakeholders to establish clear, measurable learning objectives tied to business outcomes.
- Consult with stakeholders and advise on instructional design principles, methodology, and best practices.
- Design and develop multi-channel learning experiences, integrating instructor-led, experiential, and self-paced modalities.
- Design innovative curricula utilizing adult learning theory and emerging technologies, including generative AI tools.
- Create engaging multimedia content and exercises, including video, animation, simulations, and games.
- Design scenario-based learning and immersive, interactive experiences.
- Select and apply appropriate measurement and evaluation approaches for new and existing learning programs.
- Manage instructional design projects from concept through delivery, maintaining timelines and quality.
- Maintain documentation and ensure compliance with applicable standards and requirements.

REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS
- 4+ years of instructional design experience.
- A strong portfolio demonstrating instructional design work.
- Proficiency with instructional design and e-learning tools, including Articulate Rise & Storyline, Easygenerator, and Adobe Creative Cloud.
- Familiarity with learning management systems (LMS).
- Experience designing scenario-based learning, simulations, and immersive/gamified experiences.
- Knowledge of accessibility standards and UX principles.
- Strong communication and collaboration skills, with the ability to consult and partner with stakeholders.

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
- Bachelor's or master's degree in Instructional Design, UX, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer Science, or a related field.
- Nonprofit or startup experience.
- Lived experience in or familiarity with Native and Indigenous communities (a bonus, not a requirement).

The American Indian College Fund is an equal opportunity employer. Native and Indigenous experience is a bonus and not a requirement for this role.
