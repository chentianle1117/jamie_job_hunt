Exelon — Talent Transformation Specialist

Location: Multiple-location posting. The selected candidate may be based out of one of Exelon's Corporate Office locations in IL (Chicago) or Washington, DC.
Salary Range: $64,800 – $89,100 annually, plus a 10% annual bonus for eligible positions, plus benefits.
Experience Required: 2–4 years of relevant professional experience (or 3–6 years in lieu of a bachelor's degree).

About / Role Context:
Exelon is a Fortune-200 utility holding company. The Talent Transformation Specialist supports the Talent, Learning & DEI Center of Excellence (COE). The role provides flexible project/program management and operational support across multiple Talent COE managers, supporting enterprise talent-management, culture, and inclusion initiatives.

Key Responsibilities:
- Provide flexible project and program management plus operational support across multiple Talent COE managers and initiatives.
- Independently manage assigned deliverables for enterprise talent-management, culture, and inclusion initiatives.
- Support budget and financial reporting for the Talent COE.
- Coordinate with HR Technology, HR Analytics, Finance, and external vendors.
- Support vendor coordination — tracking deliverables, invoices, and contracts.
- Coordinate stakeholder meetings and manage project deliverables and timelines.
- Develop dashboards and status reports; conduct data analysis to identify trends.
- Research best practices and emerging workplace/talent trends.
- Support process improvements using GenAI tools.
- Provide administrative coordination across multiple HR leaders.

Minimum Qualifications:
- Bachelor's degree in HR, Business, Communications, or a related field (or 3–6 years of relevant experience in lieu of a degree).
- Ability to work and manage deliverables independently.
- Strong organizational and communication skills.
- Proficiency in Microsoft Office.
- Familiarity with GenAI tools.
- Exposure to project management or analytics work.

Preferred Qualifications:
- Experience with talent management initiatives.
- Experience working in matrixed environments.
- Familiarity with HR systems (LMS, talent review platforms).
- Interest in talent strategy and data-driven decision-making.

Benefits: Medical/dental/vision insurance, 401(k) match, paid time off, tuition reimbursement, and Employee Assistance Program.
