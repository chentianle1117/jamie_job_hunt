## TL;DR — the 5 things that actually move the needle (from your own data)

1. **A true personal anchor in every message.** Your Portland-move line appears in *every* reach that
   got a reply. The Boise-route coincidence won Shannon; the same-hometown (同鄉) note warmed Phoebe.
   No anchor = the message reads like a mail-merge.
2. **Warm intros convert ~100%.** Every warm intro you've had (Nike via Amy Rapp, Boly:Welch via
   Tiffanie, Interface via Pia, Morgan Stanley via Helen) led to a chat or interview. Cold converts far
   worse. **First question for every role: is there an intro path?**
3. **One TRUE specific detail about the person** — not "noticed your HRBP experience" (that exact line
   got you 0/3 replies). Shannon converted because you said something real: *"I saw you're a certified
   Clifton Strengths coach — that's wonderful work."*
4. **Give before you ask.** Your wins open with a compliment or a shared thing. Your misses open with
   "any insights you might have on the role?" (asking a stranger for unpaid labor up front).
5. **Concrete time windows, single follow-up, then stop.** "Friday 10 or 11am PST" beats "let me know
   your availability." One nudge after 5–7 days, never a third.

## The funnel (a structural thing worth internalizing)

**LinkedIn is the cold first-touch. Email is the warm continuation.** Almost every "successful email"
in your history actually *opens* at "thanks again for agreeing to chat" — the cold contact already
happened on LinkedIn, and email just carried scheduling + your resume. So:

```
Is there a warm-intro path (someone you/David know, or a mutual connection)?
  YES → ask for the intro first. Highest conversion by far.
  NO  → Is a real email findable?
          YES → cold EMAIL (your strongest format — the C1/Blake structure below)
          NO  → LinkedIn note: specific-detail version if you have a true detail, else anchor-only
```

Cadence: **first touch → one follow-up after 5–7 days → stop.** Max ~2 touches per person, ever.
Cap cold first-touches at ~5–8 per week — quality over blast.

## Who to reach, per role (priority order)

1. **A warm-intro broker** — anyone you or David know at the company, or one connection away. *Always ask first.*
2. **A future peer / practitioner** on the People/HR/L&D/OD team (IC or manager). Low-stakes, high willingness — the "fellow practitioner" lane.
3. **The hiring manager** (who the role reports to). Highest value; use the more deferential template.
4. **A recruiter / TA partner** — agency recruiters especially *want* to place people and have replied warmly to you.
5. **Alumni** — USC (Marshall/Annenberg, MAPP/Org Psych) or Wesleyan — **only if real** on their live profile.

**Hard rule:** open the person's *live* LinkedIn, confirm current employer + title, before drafting.
We got this wrong twice before (Jessica had left Roivant; Kaitlyn was at Google not Built In). No
verification → no message.

## Banned phrases (auto-reject — these are what killed the misses)
- "any insights you might have on the role or team" ← the exact 0/3-reply fingerprint
- "I'd love to pick your brain" · "I'd love to learn more" (as a standalone)
- "I came across your profile" *without* a true specific detail right after
- "I've followed how [the org] has…" ← **fabrication**, never claim research you didn't do
- "passionate about" · "dream company" · "perfect fit" · "synergy" · "leverage" (verb) · "move the needle"
- Anything reframing ODN Oregon as "community building / ERG / network growth" (it's OD diagnostic consulting)
- The banned "$340K / 17 launches" metric — use "78% program enrollment rate"
