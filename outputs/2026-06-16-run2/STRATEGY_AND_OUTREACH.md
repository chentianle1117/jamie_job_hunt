## 2. What's been going wrong (root causes, bluntly)

1. **Targeting drifted up and out of range.** The tracker is full of *Senior* People Partner / Senior PM / Senior Consultant roles and famously selective big-tech (Stripe, Datadog, Scale AI, OpenAI, Anthropic, Figma…). For a ~3-YOE OD/People candidate who needs H-1B sponsorship, these are ~0% conversion. They consumed most of the volume.
2. **Sponsorship reality wasn't gating hard enough.** Many targets don't sponsor junior People/HR roles at all (the lottery + cost makes a 3-YOE coordinator a hard sell at most). Cap-exempt employers (universities, hospitals, nonprofits) were under-weighted despite being structurally easier.
3. **Volume crowded out warmth.** The auto-apply machine sprayed; the warm channel (a human email per role) — which is the *only* thing that's worked — was treated as an afterthought.
4. **A self-targeting tension:** roles like C1 came back "you seem overqualified" for the *coordinator* level, while *Senior* roles reject as *under*-qualified. Jamie sits in a real squeeze — the fix is precise level-matching (Coordinator/Specialist/Generalist/Associate), not spraying both ends.

---

## 3. The reset — new operating model (70/30, warm-first)

**Stop:** auto-applying to Senior titles, big-tech-volume roles, and anything with no plausible sponsorship.
**Start:** a smaller, sharper funnel where every application is at-level + sponsor-plausible, and most get a *human touch*.

**Target box (apply only if it fits):**
- **Level:** Coordinator / Specialist / Generalist / Associate / Analyst. (No "Senior", no "Manager" at non-consulting firms, no "Lead.")
- **Function:** People Ops, L&D, OD, Talent Coordination, Employee Experience, HR Generalist (junior), Onboarding.
- **Sponsorship:** plausible — prioritize (a) **cap-exempt** (universities/hospitals/nonprofits — H-1B without the lottery), (b) large established sponsors, (c) Portland-local (she's physically there, less competition).
- **Geography:** Portland-local **first**, remote-US second.

**The 70/30 split:**
- **70% warm:** for each fitting role, find one human (recruiter, hiring manager, or alum) and send a short personal note — the C1/Nike move, which is the only thing that's generated warmth. Plus nurture every live thread.
- **30% auto:** a *small* stream of genuinely-fitting no-account applications (the mechanics work; just point them at the right targets).

**Weekly cadence (realistic, ~5 hrs/wk):**
- Mon: pick 5–8 fitting roles (cap-exempt + Portland + at-level). 
- Tue–Wed: apply + send 5–8 warm notes (1 human per role).
- Thu: follow up on anything >5 days silent (the C1 lesson: follow up *once*, then let go).
- Fri: nurture live threads (interviews, Nike, etc.) + log feedback.

## 4. Outreach playbook (reusable)

**Principles (from what worked):** short (<120 words), specific to the company, lead with *fit not need*, sponsorship mentioned only if natural and never first, always end with a low-friction ask. Jamie's own C1/Nike notes are the proven voice — warm, concrete, a little personal (the "roses blooming in Portland" line landed).

**When to use which:**
- **Post-application nudge** → after applying to a fitting role, email the recruiter/HM directly (this is what got C1 + More Than Models moving).
- **Warm/alum** → for a 2nd-degree or school connection; ask for a 15-min chat, not a referral.
- **Follow-up** → once, ~5–7 days after silence. Then stop (don't 4x-chase like the C1 thread).
- **Post-rejection relationship-bank** → reply graciously, ask to stay in touch (Brex/Diane offered exactly this).
